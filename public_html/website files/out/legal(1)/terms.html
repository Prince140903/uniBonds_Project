4.4 Refund and Cancellation:

As the Platform operates as an online bond trading interface, refunds or cancellations are not applicable once an order has been successfully placed and settled. In cases where an order has been placed with the exchange but has not been settled, and payment has already been made by the user, the clearing corporation will initiate an automatic refund to the user's original source account.

Additionally, the following procedures apply to trades that have been placed but remain unsettled at the clearing corporation:

  1. Requests for trade cancellation or modification will be considered by the exchange only if both transacting parties consent and submit appropriate formal requests. A valid reason for the request must be provided by the participants.
  2. Such requests must be submitted by authorized individuals, including the Risk Officer, Compliance Officer, Chief Investment Officer, or Head of Treasury of the respective participant.
  3. Trade cancellations or modifications may be permitted in specific cases such as input errors (fat finger), pricing mismatches due to incorrect cash flow assumptions, short selling, shut periods, or other similar circumstances, provided that both parties agree. These requests will only be processed if the complete and accurate documentation is received within 60 minutes of market close. The designated time windows for RFQ deal cancellation/modification requests are:
    1. T+0 Settlement: Up to 5:15 PM
    2. T+1 Settlement: Up to 6:15 PM
  4. For RFQ deal cancellations or modifications involving trades of ₹50 lakhs or less (face value terms)—a measure intended to promote retail investor participation—requests will be allowed up until 5:00 PM on the settlement day. Accordingly, the updated timelines for such RFQ deals are:
    1. T+0 Settlement: Until 5:15 PM on the settlement day
    2. T+1 Settlement: Until 5:00 PM on the settlement day
  5. Modifications are permitted only for specific parameters as determined by the Exchange from time to time. Initially, permissible changes may include settlement start date (T+0/T+1), accrued interest, yield start, and total consideration.
  6. Non-individual participants may request up to three modifications or cancellations per quarter, while individual investors (identified via PAN) are allowed up to three instances per month. If a participant exceeds these limits, their access to the RFQ platform will be disabled for 15 calendar days. Modification and cancellation requests will be tracked separately. In cases of settlement failure, further actions—including platform disablement—will be undertaken as per SEBI's approval.
  7. For RFQ deal modification or cancellation, the participant who initiates the request will be considered the "Initiator", and only their instance count will be recorded. The counterpart ("Responder") will not have their instance count affected.
  8. Changes to Custody details, Bank Account, Demat Account, and total consideration (within a tolerance of ± ₹100) will be permitted without limitation on the number of instances.
  9. The Exchange will approve cancellation or modification requests only if all specified conditions are satisfied.
  10. Once a trade modification or cancellation is approved, the relevant updates will be reflected on the RFQ platform accordingly.
  11. The Exchange will furnish any information relating to trade cancellations or modifications to the Regulator, upon request.
  12. Details of such instances will also be periodically reported to the designated Committee by the Exchange.
  13. As part of the Platform's service use, you may be required to provide your mobile number. By doing so, you confirm that the mobile number is registered in your name and under your control.
  14. For exceptions or support related to trade cancellation/modification, you may contact us via email at support@unibonds.in .
